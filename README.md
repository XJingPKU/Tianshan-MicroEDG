# Tianshan-MicroEDG (Elevational diversity gradients)

This repository contains data and code for the manuscript entitled "Soil depth governs microbial biogeography and intensifies environmental filtering along elevational gradients" by Maoping Li, Wenting Feng, Yuan Chen, Jinjiao Hu, Junpeng Rui, Congcong Shen, Chao Song, Kai Sun, Yuanxiao Xu, Yugang Wang, Xin Jing

**Abstract**  
Soil depth is a critical yet overlooked dimension in microbial macroecology. By integrating a 3-km elevational transect on the Tianshan Mountains with a meta-analysis of 29 global studies, we demonstrate that soil depth fundamentally reshapes microbial biogeographic responses to elevation. While elevational diversity patterns exist across the profile, their intensity increases significantly with depth. Mechanistically, we find that environmental filtering explains a greater proportion of diversity variance in deep layers, suggesting that subsurface microbes are subject to more stringent ecological constraints than surface ones. These results indicate that surface-centric assessments systematically underestimate microbial vulnerability to environmental shifts. Ignoring the vertical dimension introduces fundamental biases into our understanding of soil biodiversity. We argue that integrating deep-soil dynamics into a macroecological framework is essential for predicting the resilience of terrestrial ecosystems under global change.
